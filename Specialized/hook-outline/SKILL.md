# Hook Outline

Turn incomplete source material into a creator-ready draft with two things: a complete first-person intro written exactly as it should be spoken, and a compact outline the creator can follow while filming. Do not expand into a full script, production plan, shot list, or research memo unless requested.

## Source boundary

Use only the user's message, attached text, referenced page, transcript, draft, or verified research needed for the task. Separate source facts, creator opinions, and unknowns. Preserve important claims, examples, names, products, numbers, and links. Never invent evidence, quotes, product behavior, timelines, metrics, results, or personal experience.

Do not embellish thin notes with unsupported adjectives, causal explanations, or assumed audience behavior. If a useful bridge is interpretive, keep it explicitly in the creator's voice with phrasing such as "I think" or omit it.

## Core promise

Reduce the material to one sentence: This video shows [specific audience] why or how [concrete subject] creates [clear consequence or useful payoff]. Use that promise to remove side quests.

## Spoken intro

Write camera-ready first-person copy using: hook, context, credibility or proof, stakes, promise, and transition. Begin with the strongest concrete claim, tension, object, result, or contradiction. When supported, prefer a causal opening that connects a concrete result or contradiction to why the video matters. This creates more tension than starting with a broad definition.

Keep the hook credible and curiosity-driven. Treat the intro as a cold open: state the tension, stakes, and promise once, then stop before the energy drops.

Use short paragraphs, direct language, contractions, and natural rhythm. Remove dictation scaffolding such as "here's the intro," "I'm going to say," and "the point of this video is." Avoid robotic fragments, multi-breath sentences, filler, repetition, and accidental meta-commentary.

## Outline

Default to 8 to 10 one-line bullets for long-form videos, fewer for narrow sources. Sequence from concrete to conceptual: demo or object, proof or context, mechanism, comparisons or limitations, lesson or implication, resolution of the opening promise.

Write bullets as filmable actions or story beats, not mini-essays or topic labels. Start with a concrete verb whenever possible: show, run, compare, test, explain, reveal, or close. Each bullet should tell the creator what to do or cover next.

When the premise compares products, platforms, versions, or outcomes, reserve a distinct side-by-side comparison beat before drawing the conclusion. Avoid nested bullets unless requested.

## Research and links

Place each source link directly on the intro line or outline bullet it supports. When research informed the draft, attach an inline primary source to every factual claim, number, quote, benchmark, price, timeline, availability statement, or product detail. Remove or clearly frame unsupported claims. Do not add a bibliography unless requested.

## Destination

Return the draft in chat unless the user asks to save it elsewhere. When a destination is requested, use the connected tool, verify the artifact, and return its link. For video databases, use Long Form for YouTube outlines, Short Form for reels or shorts, In progress only when explicitly stated, otherwise Idea. Put the full draft in the body.

## Default output

## Intro

[Complete first-person spoken intro in short paragraphs.]

## Outline

- [Concrete beat 1.]
- [Proof or context beat 2.]
- [Mechanism beat 3.]
- [Further beats as needed.]

Do not prepend process commentary.

## Final quality gate

Confirm that the opening is concrete and causal only when supported, the intro is fully speakable, the intro ends with a promise or transition, the cold open does not overstay its welcome, the outline delivers that promise logically, every bullet is concise, verb-led, and actionable, comparison-driven videos contain a dedicated synthesis beat, source details survive, no unsupported adjectives, facts, causal claims, metrics, results, or fake experience appear, every researched factual beat has an inline source, and the deliverable remains an intro plus concise outline. Revise failures before returning.
